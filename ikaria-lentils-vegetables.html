Ikaria-Style Lentils with Vegetables

A rustic Ikaria staple of lentils simmered with vegetables, herbs, and olive oil.

Main Dishes Stovetop Yield: 6 servings

Ingredients
  • 1½ cups dried brown or green lentils, rinsed
  • 6 cups water or low-sodium vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • Red wine vinegar or lemon juice, to taste
Procedure
  1. In a large pot, combine lentils and water or broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 20 minutes, until lentils are tender.
  2. While lentils cook, heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add onion, carrots, and celery and sauté until softened, about 6–8 minutes.
  3. Add garlic and oregano to the vegetables and cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Stir the vegetable mixture and bay leaf into the pot of lentils.
  5. Continue simmering uncovered for 10–15 minutes, allowing flavors to meld.
  6. Season with salt and pepper. Remove bay leaf.
  7. Finish with parsley and a splash of red wine vinegar or lemon juice before serving.
