Galway Pie Maker – Slow-Braised Beef & Stout Pie

Single-serve beef and stout pie with a buttery double crust and optional wild rice variation.

Ingredients
Filling (enough for 1–2 small pies)
  • ½ tablespoon olive oil or butter
  • 4 oz beef chuck or stew meat, cut into ¾-inch cubes (or ground beef)
  • 2 tablespoons onion, finely chopped
  • 1 small clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon carrot, finely diced (optional)
  • ¼ cup Irish stout (such as Guinness)
  • ¼ cup low-sodium beef stock
  • ½ teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • ¼ teaspoon fresh thyme (or a pinch dried)
  • 1 small bay leaf (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon cooked wild rice (optional)
  • ½ teaspoon cornstarch mixed with 1 teaspoon water
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Small pat of butter, to finish

Use ready-made pastry: shortcrust for the base and puff or shortcrust for the lid.

Procedure
Prepare filling and cook in pie maker
  1. Heat oil in a small skillet over medium-high heat. Brown the beef for about 3 minutes and season lightly.
  2. Add onion, garlic, and carrot and cook for 2–3 minutes until softened.
  3. Deglaze the pan with stout, scraping up any browned bits.
  4. Add beef stock, Worcestershire sauce, thyme, and bay leaf. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 20–25 minutes, or until beef is tender.
  5. Stir in cooked wild rice if using. Add cornstarch slurry and simmer 3–5 minutes until thick and glossy.
  6. Remove bay leaf, season to taste, and stir in a small pat of butter. Cool filling for 10–15 minutes.
  7. Preheat the pie maker. Line with bottom crust, add ¼–⅓ cup filling, top with lid crust, seal edges, vent, and brush with egg wash.
  8. Cook 8–12 minutes until deep golden. Rest 2–3 minutes before removing.
Notes: Inspired by Galway’s “Slow Braised Beef & Stout” pies. Wild rice reflects a personal variation; pearl barley may be substituted for a more traditional Irish grain.
