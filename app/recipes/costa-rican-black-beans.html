Costa Rican Black Beans

A Nicoya Peninsula staple of slow-simmered black beans with simple aromatics.

Beans Stovetop 90 Minutes

Ingredients
Traditional Nicoyan preparation
  • 1 pound dried black beans
  • 8 cups water (for soaking)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt to taste
Procedure
Slow cooking builds depth of flavor
  1. Rinse beans and soak overnight in water. Drain before cooking.
  2. Place soaked beans in a large pot, cover with fresh water, bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until tender, about 60–90 minutes.
  3. Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat. Sauté onion, bell pepper, and garlic until softened.
  4. Add cumin and oregano to the vegetables and cook briefly until fragrant.
  5. Stir the vegetable mixture into the cooked beans and simmer together for 10–15 minutes.
  6. Season with salt to taste and serve warm.
