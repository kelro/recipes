Costa Rican Gallo Pinto

Costa Rica’s national dish of rice and black beans, traditionally eaten for breakfast or lunch.

Main Dishes Stovetop Yield: 6 servings

Ingredients
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups cooked black beans, drained (reserve some cooking liquid)
  • 2 cups cooked brown rice
  • ½ cup reserved bean cooking liquid or water
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
Procedure
  1. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onion, bell pepper, and garlic and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Stir in black beans, cumin, salt, and pepper. Cook for 2–3 minutes.
  3. Add cooked rice and reserved bean liquid. Stir well to combine.
  4. Reduce heat to low and cook, stirring occasionally, until heated through and slightly creamy, about 5 minutes.
  5. Remove from heat and stir in chopped cilantro.
  6. Serve warm on its own or alongside vegetables or fruit, as traditionally eaten in Costa Rica.
