Split Pea Soup With Ham

Hearty one-pot comfort soup using ham and green split peas.

Ingredients
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cups diced ham
  • 1 yellow onion, chopped
  • 3 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 3 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 1 lb dried green split peas, rinsed
  • 8 cups chicken stock
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper, plus more to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley (optional, for serving)
Procedure
  1. Heat a large Dutch oven or soup pot over medium heat. Add the olive oil and diced ham. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the ham is lightly browned, about 8–10 minutes.
  2. Increase heat to medium-high; add the onion, carrots, and celery. Continue cooking until the vegetables begin to soften, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute more.
  3. Meanwhile, rinse the split peas thoroughly in a colander and discard any debris.
  4. Add the rinsed split peas, chicken stock, dried thyme, and bay leaf (if using) to the pot. Bring to a simmer.
  5. Reduce heat to medium-low and cook, uncovered, stirring occasionally, until the split peas open and become very tender, about 1 hour 30 minutes. If the soup becomes too thick, add ½ to 1 cup of water to reach your desired consistency.
  6. Season with additional salt and black pepper to taste. Serve hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley if desired.
