Miso Soup with Vegetables

A light Okinawan-inspired miso soup with tofu, shiitake mushrooms, and greens.

Soup Stovetop ~15 minutes

Ingredients
For the miso soup with vegetables
  • 3 tablespoons miso paste (shiro, red, or shinshu)
  • 1½ tablespoons unseasoned rice wine vinegar
  • 1 large garlic clove, peeled
  • 1½-inch piece fresh ginger, peeled
  • ½ pound firm tofu, cut into ½-inch cubes
  • ¼ pound fresh shiitake mushrooms, stems removed and caps thinly sliced
  • 2 cups pea shoots (about 3 oz), roughly chopped
  • 6 medium scallions, trimmed and thinly chopped
  • 2 teaspoons toasted sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
Procedure
Prepare the soup
  1. Place miso paste, rice vinegar, garlic, ginger, and 1 cup of water in a food processor or blender. Blend until smooth, scraping down the sides as needed.
  2. Pour the miso mixture into a medium saucepan with 4 additional cups of water and stir to combine.
  3. Add tofu, shiitake mushrooms, pea shoots, and scallions; bring to a simmer over medium heat, stirring often.
  4. Reduce heat to low and simmer uncovered for about 5 minutes.
  5. Turn off heat and stir in sesame oil and soy sauce before serving.
Notes: This soup is enjoyed year-round and tastes best served hot. If you prefer more texture, mince the garlic and ginger and add them directly in step 3 instead of blending them. Substitute baby spinach or watercress for the pea shoots if needed.
