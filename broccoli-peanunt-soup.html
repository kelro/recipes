Broccoli Peanut Soup

Luscious and nutty soup with the rich flavor of peanut butter and vibrant broccoli.

Soups & Stews Stovetop Yield: 6 servings

Ingredients
Warm, savory, and a touch of sweetness
  • 1½ tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 large onions, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 medium carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 32-ounce carton vegetable broth (or 4 cups water + 2 vegetable bouillon cubes)
  • 2 medium apples, peeled, cored, and diced
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • ⅔ cup creamy peanut butter
  • 6 heaping cups finely chopped broccoli (florets + tender stems)
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper to taste
  • Pinch of dried hot red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Chopped roasted peanuts for garnish (optional)
Procedure
Sauté, simmer, blend, and finish with broccoli & lemon
  1. Heat olive oil in a soup pot over medium-low heat. Add onions and sauté until translucent; add garlic and carrots and cook until golden.
  2. Add vegetable broth, apples, and curry powder. Bring to a slow boil, then reduce heat; simmer gently 10–15 minutes, until carrots and apples are tender. Remove from heat.
  3. Transfer solid ingredients to a food processor and pulse until just coarsely pureed (or use an immersion blender directly in the pot, leaving some texture).
  4. Return mixture to the pot. Add peanut butter gradually, whisking until fully blended. Return to very low heat.
  5. Steam broccoli with about ¼ cup water, covered, 5 minutes until bright green and tender-crisp. Stir into the soup.
  6. If the soup is too thick, add additional broth or water to reach desired consistency. Stir in lemon juice and season with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with chopped peanuts if desired.
