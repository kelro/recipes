Empanadas

Classic baked empanadas filled with seasoned ground beef, olives, eggs, and herbs.

Ingredients
For the filling and dough

For the Filling

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 white onions, chopped (about 4 cups)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ red bell pepper, diced
  • 1½ teaspoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 2 tablespoons sweet paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon beef bouillon
  • 1 pound lean ground beef
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 bunch green onions, green parts only, finely chopped
  • ½ cup chopped green olives
  • ½ cup fresh chopped parsley
  • 3 hard-boiled eggs, peeled and finely chopped

For the Dough (Makes ~20 Empanadas)

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • ¾ to 1 cup warm water
  • Egg wash (1 egg mixed with 1 tablespoon water)
Procedure
Prepare filling, dough, and bake

Prepare the Filling

  1. Heat olive oil over medium-low heat. Add onions and cook, stirring often, for about 10 minutes.
  2. Add garlic and diced red bell pepper. Cook another 10 minutes, stirring often.
  3. Add chili powder, cumin, sweet paprika, and oregano. Cook for 2 minutes to bloom the spices.
  4. Increase heat to medium and add ground beef. Season with salt and pepper and cook, breaking into small pieces, until browned. Stir in beef bouillon.
  5. Remove from heat and transfer mixture to a bowl. Let cool for 10 minutes, then stir in green onions, olives, parsley, and chopped eggs.
  6. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (or up to 3 days). Filling must be well chilled before assembling.

Prepare the Dough

  1. In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Add butter and beaten egg and mix until incorporated.
  2. Add oil and warm water, stirring until a dough forms. Add more water if dough feels dry.
  3. Transfer dough to a lightly greased bowl, cover, and rest at room temperature for 1 hour.

Assemble and Bake

  1. Divide dough into two portions. Roll each into a large circle about ⅛-inch thick.
  2. Cut dough into 6-inch circles for regular empanadas or 3-inch circles for mini empanadas.
  3. Place about 2 tablespoons of filling in the center of each circle.
  4. Moisten edges with water, fold into a half-moon shape, and seal edges by crimping or twisting.
  5. Place empanadas on a parchment-lined baking sheet. Brush with egg wash.
  6. Bake at 375 °F for 25–30 minutes, until golden brown and fully cooked.
Notes: Chilling the filling is essential to prevent leaking during baking. Empanadas can be assembled ahead and refrigerated or frozen before baking. Serve warm with chimichurri, salsa, or a simple green salad.
