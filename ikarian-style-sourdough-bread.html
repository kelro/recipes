Ikarian-Style Sourdough Bread

Traditional Ikarian sourdough loaf made with wild yeast, whole wheat and semolina for a hearty bread.

Breads Baked Approx. 3 hrs

Ingredients
For the Ikarian sourdough loaf
  • 2 cups sourdough starter (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup room-temperature water
  • 3⅓ cups (1 pound) whole wheat flour
  • 2¾ cups (1 pound) semolina flour
  • Pinch of salt and pepper
  • Sesame seeds, optional
Procedure
Mix, rise, and bake
  1. In a very large bowl, mix the sourdough starter and water until combined.
  2. In another bowl, combine whole wheat flour, semolina flour, salt, and pepper.
  3. Add the flour mixture to the water mixture about 1 cup at a time, stirring thoroughly as you go. Use your hands as needed until a cohesive dough forms.
  4. Knead the dough for at least 20 minutes until elastic and smooth.
  5. Place the dough in a low, round 20-inch pan, cover with a drizzle of olive oil, and sprinkle sesame seeds if desired.
  6. Cut a small cross in the center of the dough, cover with a clean towel, and let rise for about 2 hours.
  7. Preheat the oven. Bake at 450 °F (230 °C) for 20 minutes, then at 390 °F (200 °C) for 20 minutes, and finally at 350 °F (175 °C) for 20 minutes for a well-baked loaf.
Tips: If the dough feels too dry while mixing, add small amounts of water a little at a time until it becomes supple and workable.
