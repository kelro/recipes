Hot Toddy

A classic warm beverage of hot water, honey, lemon, and whiskey — perfect for cozy evenings or soothing a scratchy throat.

Beverages Stovetop ~10 minutes

Ingredients
For one hot toddy
  • ¾ cup hot water
  • 1½ ounces whiskey (or bourbon)
  • 2–3 teaspoons honey, to taste
  • 2–3 teaspoons lemon juice, to taste
  • 1 slice lemon
  • Optional: 1 cinnamon stick or a pinch of cloves
Procedure
Stir and serve warm
  1. Bring water to a simmer in a kettle or small saucepan.
  2. Pour hot water into a mug and add whiskey, honey, and lemon juice.
  3. Stir until honey dissolves.
  4. Add a lemon slice and, if desired, garnish with a cinnamon stick or cloves.
  5. Sip slowly while warm and enjoy the comforting flavors.
Notes: A hot toddy is a classic warm drink often enjoyed in cold weather or when your throat feels irritated. If you prefer a non-alcoholic version, substitute herbal tea (such as ginger or chamomile) for the water, and omit the whiskey. Honey soothes the throat and lemon adds vitamin C and brightness to the drink. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}
