Spanish Paella with Chicken and Seafood

A vibrant paella mixta with saffron-infused rice, tender chicken, shrimp, calamari, mussels, and clams.

Ingredients
Paella mixta essentials
  • 4 cups seafood stock
  • 8 saffron threads
  • 6 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 8 jumbo shrimp, shelled and deveined
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • ½ lb calamari, cleaned and cut into rings and pieces (about 227g)
  • 2/3 cup peas (fresh or frozen)
  • ¾ cup chopped green beans (cut into 1-inch pieces)
  • 1 cup canned crushed tomatoes
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1¾ cups Spanish bomba rice (about 360g)
  • ½ lb fresh mussels, scrubbed and debearded
  • ½ lb fresh clams, purged and scrubbed
  • ½ teaspoon smoked Spanish paprika
Procedure
Layered cooking for depth of flavor
  1. Warm seafood stock with saffron threads in a saucepan over medium heat; reduce heat to low to keep warm.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large paella pan or wide heavy skillet over medium-high heat. Season shrimp with salt and pepper and sear about 1 minute per side; transfer to a plate and set aside.
  3. Season chicken with salt and pepper and brown in the same pan, about 5 minutes; transfer to a plate and set aside.
  4. Add onion and calamari to the pan; cook until onion begins to brown, about 7 minutes. Stir in peas and green beans for 2 minutes more.
  5. Add crushed tomatoes and garlic; cook until tomato reduces by half (about 2 minutes).
  6. Sprinkle rice, mussels, clams, smoked paprika, and reserved chicken evenly in the pan; stir briefly to coat the rice with oil and ingredients.
  7. Pour in warm stock and add a pinch of salt. Cook without stirring for 5 minutes over medium heat.
  8. Reduce heat to low and simmer 10–15 minutes, until rice has absorbed most of the stock and is tender.
  9. About 3 minutes before finished, nestle the reserved shrimp on top of the rice to cook through.
  10. Remove from heat and let rest about 3 minutes before serving.
Notes: You don’t need a traditional paella pan—a large, wide skillet works well. Letting the rice absorb the stock without stirring helps develop the flavorful texture. Serve with lemon wedges and simple tapas to round out the meal.
