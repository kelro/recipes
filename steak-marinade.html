Steak Marinade

A quick balsamic-based marinade for juicy, flavorful steaks.

Ingredients
Marinade for 4–6 steaks
  • 4–6 (8 oz) steaks
  • 1/3 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1/3 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup oil
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon dried Italian seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried mustard
  • Chopped parsley, for garnish (optional)
Procedure
Grill method (default)
  1. Whisk together balsamic vinegar, soy sauce, oil, Worcestershire sauce, honey, Italian seasoning, garlic powder, and dried mustard until combined.
  2. Place steaks and marinade in a zip-top bag. Toss to coat evenly, press out excess air, seal, and refrigerate for 15–20 minutes.
  3. Remove steaks from marinade and pat dry with paper towels.
  4. Grill over medium-high heat until desired doneness is reached.
  5. Remove from heat and rest steaks for 5 minutes before serving. Garnish with parsley if desired.
Notes: Do not marinate longer than 30 minutes. The acid in the marinade can break down the meat too much if left longer.

Cooking Variations — Cast-Iron Pan Fry:
For indoor cooking, use a heavy cast-iron skillet for best results. After marinating, remove steaks and pat completely dry. Heat skillet over medium-high to high heat and add 1–2 tablespoons high-smoke-point oil (avocado or canola). Cook steaks without crowding and flip once. Rest 5 minutes before serving.

Pan-Fry Times (Approximate):
  • Ribeye (1–1½ inch): 3–4 minutes per side for medium-rare. Ribeye’s higher fat content tolerates slightly longer cooking.
  • New York Strip (1–1½ inch): 3 minutes per side for medium-rare. Avoid overcooking to keep it tender.
  • Sirloin (1–1½ inch): 2½–3 minutes per side for medium-rare. Sirloin is leaner and cooks faster.
Optional: Add 1 tablespoon butter during the last minute of cooking and baste steaks for extra richness.
