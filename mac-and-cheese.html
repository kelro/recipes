Ingredients
Stovetop creamy — with an optional baked topping.
  • 8 oz macaroni (about 2 cups dry), or other small pasta of choice
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp dry mustard powder
  • 1.5 cups milk of choice
  • 1/2 lb Velveeta cheese, cubed
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar

Optional Baked Topping

  • 1/4 cup shredded cheddar
  • 1/4 cup panko breadcrumbs or crushed Ritz crackers
Procedure
Make sauce, add pasta, serve — or bake for a crispy top.
  1. Cook the macaroni in a large pot of salted water according to package directions; drain well.
  2. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. When melted, stir in the flour, garlic powder, onion powder, and dry mustard until smooth and incorporated.
  3. Pour the milk into the pan in a thin stream while whisking until smooth.
  4. Keep on medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sauce bubbles and thickens, about 3–5 minutes.
  5. Add the cubed Velveeta and shredded cheddar; stir until melted and creamy.
  6. Add the drained macaroni and mix until evenly coated. Taste and season with salt and pepper.
  7. Optional bake: Spoon into an oven-safe casserole dish. Top with 1/4 cup shredded cheddar and 1/4 cup panko breadcrumbs (or crushed Ritz). Bake at 350°F until melted and golden/crisp, about 20–30 minutes.
Notes: The stovetop version is ready as soon as the pasta is coated. Baking is purely for that golden, crunchy top.
