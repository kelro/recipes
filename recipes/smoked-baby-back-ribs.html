Electric Smoker Baby Back Ribs (3 lbs)

Tender, smoky baby backs using a modified 3-2-1 method that works great in an electric smoker.

Yield: 3 lbs baby back ribs (about 2–4 servings)
Prep: 15–20 min
Cook: ~4.5–5 hrs
Smoker Temp: 225°F (optional finish 250°F)

Ingredients

Ribs

  • 3 lbs baby back pork ribs
  • Binder (optional): 1–2 tbsp yellow mustard or olive oil or thin BBQ sauce

Dry Rub (for ~3 lbs)

  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tsp kosher salt
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp paprika (smoked if available)
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • ½ tsp chili powder or cayenne (optional)

Foil Wrap Add-ins (during wrap phase)

  • 2–3 tbsp butter (cut into pats)
  • 1–2 tbsp brown sugar
  • Optional splash: 1–2 tbsp apple juice or apple cider vinegar
  • Optional: 1 tbsp honey

Optional Finish

  • BBQ sauce (to brush on during final phase)

Equipment

  • Electric smoker
  • Wood chips: apple or cherry (hickory also works, go light)
  • Heavy-duty aluminum foil
  • Paper towels + butter knife (for membrane removal)
  • Instant-read thermometer (recommended)

Prep

  1. Remove the membrane (bone side). Slide a butter knife under the silvery membrane, grab with a paper towel, and pull it off.
  2. Apply binder (optional). Lightly coat both sides with mustard (classic), oil, or thin BBQ sauce.
  3. Season. Mix the rub and apply generously to both sides.
  4. Rest the ribs. Minimum: 30 minutes at room temp. Best: wrap and refrigerate 2–12 hours (then pull out ~30 min before smoking).

Smoker Setup

  1. Preheat smoker to 225°F.
  2. Use a water pan (helps keep ribs juicy).
  3. Add wood chips: apple or cherry are ideal for baby backs.

Cook (Modified 3-2-1 for Baby Backs)

Phase 1 — Smoke (2 hours @ 225°F)

  1. Place ribs in smoker bone-side down.
  2. Run smoke primarily during the first 60–90 minutes.
  3. Avoid frequent peeking (electric smokers recover heat slowly).

Phase 2 — Wrap (1½ hours @ 225°F)

  1. Lay ribs on heavy-duty foil.
  2. Add butter pats + brown sugar (and optional splash of apple juice/vinegar, optional honey).
  3. Wrap tightly and return to smoker for 1½ hours.

Phase 3 — Unwrap & Finish (1 to 1½ hours @ 225°F)

  1. Carefully unwrap (hot steam + juices).
  2. Return ribs to smoker to firm up the bark.
  3. Optional: brush with BBQ sauce during the last 20–30 minutes to set.
  4. Optional: bump smoker to 250°F for the last ~20 minutes to tighten the exterior.

Doneness Checks

  • Temp: typically 195–203°F near the thickest meat (avoid touching bone)
  • Bend test: lift with tongs — ribs should bend easily and the bark may crack slightly
  • Visual: meat pulls back from bones ~½ inch

Rest & Serve

  1. Tent loosely with foil and rest 20–30 minutes.
  2. Slice between bones.
  3. Serve with sauce on the side (or a light brush if you prefer).

Notes

  • More “bite-through”: shorten the wrap phase to ~60–75 minutes.
  • More “fall-apart”: keep wrap at 1½ hours and extend final phase slightly.
  • Less sweet: reduce brown sugar in the rub to 1 tbsp and omit brown sugar in the wrap.

Source: Personal kitchen method (electric smoker baby backs; modified 3-2-1).
