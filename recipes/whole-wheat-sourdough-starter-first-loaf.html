Whole-Wheat Sourdough: Starter to First Loaf

A complete guide to building a whole-wheat sourdough starter and baking your first loaf.

Ingredients
Starter + dough formula

Starter

  • Whole-wheat flour
  • Water

Dough (100% Whole Wheat)

  • 500 g whole-wheat flour (about 4 cups)
  • 350–375 g water (about 1½ to 1⅔ cups)
  • 100 g active sourdough starter (about ½ cup)
  • 10 g fine salt (about 1¾ teaspoons)
Procedure
From starter to baked loaf
  1. Make the starter (Days 1–7): Mix 60 g whole-wheat flour with 60 g water in a clean jar. Cover loosely and keep at room temperature. Each day, stir, discard half, and feed again with 60 g flour and 60 g water.
  2. Starter readiness: The starter is ready when it doubles in 4–8 hours after feeding, appears bubbly, and smells pleasantly tangy.
  3. Autolyse (30 minutes): Mix flour with 330 g water until no dry bits remain. Cover and rest.
  4. Add starter and salt: Add 100 g starter, 10 g salt, and remaining water to reach desired hydration. Mix thoroughly.
  5. Bulk fermentation: Over 3½–6 hours, perform three sets of stretch-and-folds during the first 90 minutes, then allow the dough to rise until puffy and about 50% increased in volume.
  6. Pre-shape and shape: Shape dough into a round, rest briefly, then shape again and place seam-side up in a floured basket.
  7. Cold proof: Refrigerate covered for 8–14 hours.
  8. Bake: Preheat Dutch oven to 475°F. Bake covered for 20 minutes, then uncover, reduce to 450°F, and bake 20–25 minutes more until deeply browned.
  9. Cool completely: Allow at least 1 hour before slicing.
Notes

Dough should feel soft and slightly tacky, not stiff or soupy. Bulk fermentation is complete when dough shows bubbles and gentle wobble. Internal bake temperature should reach 205–210°F.
