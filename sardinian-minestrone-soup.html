Sardinian Minestrone Soup

A rustic vegetable and bean soup inspired by the Blue Zone of Sardinia.

Ingredients
Hearty vegetables and beans
  • 1 cup dried garbanzo beans, soaked overnight
  • 1 cup dried white beans, soaked overnight
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup potatoes, diced
  • 1 can (14 oz) chopped tomatoes
  • 6–8 cups water or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt to taste
Procedure
Slow simmer, better next day
  1. Drain soaked beans and rinse well.
  2. In a large pot, sauté onion, carrots, celery, and garlic in a small amount of water or broth until softened.
  3. Add beans, potatoes, tomatoes, oregano, bay leaf, and water or broth.
  4. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer gently for 60–90 minutes, until beans are tender.
  5. Season with salt to taste and remove bay leaf before serving.
