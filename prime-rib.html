Ingredients
Simple seasoning lets the beef shine.
  • Prime rib roast (bone-in or boneless)
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Garlic, minced (optional)
  • Fresh herbs such as rosemary or thyme (optional)
  • Olive oil or butter
Preparation
Set yourself up for even cooking.
  1. Choose the roast: Plan for about 1 pound per person (including the bone).
  2. Bring to room temperature: Remove the roast from the refrigerator 2–3 hours before cooking.
  3. Season generously:
    • Pat the roast dry with paper towels.
    • Coat lightly with olive oil or butter.
    • Rub generously with salt, pepper, and optional garlic and herbs.
  4. Preheat oven: Preheat to 450°F (232°C).
Cooking Instructions
High heat first, then slow roast.
  1. Place the roast on a rack in a roasting pan.
  2. Roast at 450°F (232°C) for 20 minutes to develop a crust.
  3. Reduce oven temperature to 325°F (163°C).
  4. Continue cooking until internal temperature reaches:
    • Rare: 120°F (49°C)
    • Medium Rare: 130°F (54°C)
    • Medium: 140°F (60°C)

    Rule of thumb: ~15 minutes per pound at 325°F.

  5. Remove roast from oven and tent loosely with foil.
  6. Let rest 20–30 minutes. Temperature will rise another 5–10°F.
Carving
Simple and traditional.
  1. If bone-in, cut the bones away first.
  2. Slice the roast into thick slabs and serve.
Optional Extras
Classic accompaniments.
  • Au Jus: Deglaze the roasting pan with beef broth and wine, scraping up browned bits. Simmer and season.
  • Horseradish Sauce: Mix sour cream, prepared horseradish, lemon juice, and salt to taste.
