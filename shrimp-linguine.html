Shrimp Linguine

Classic shrimp linguine with a garlic-cream herb sauce, perfect for a quick dinner.

Main Dishes Stovetop ~25 minutes

Ingredients
For the shrimp linguine
  • 1½ pounds cooked fresh shrimp
  • 6 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • ½ cup chopped parsley
  • 3 tablespoons chopped fresh basil
  • ½ teaspoon dried thyme
  • ¾ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt, to taste
  • Ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 (16 ounce) package linguine pasta
Procedure
Cook pasta and sauce
  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook linguine until tender yet firm to the bite, about 11 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, melt the butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add garlic and sauté briefly to release the flavor.
  3. Add the cream and heat just to boiling, stirring frequently. Reduce the heat to low.
  4. Stir in shrimp, parsley, basil, and thyme. Continue cooking until the shrimp are just heated through, then remove the sauce from heat.
  5. Drain the pasta and toss lightly with the cream sauce. Add Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper to taste.
  6. Toss again and serve immediately.
Notes:
This shrimp linguine is a quick, satisfying pasta dinner with a creamy garlic sauce and fresh herbs. To lighten the sauce, substitute half-and-half for the heavy cream.
