Whole-Wheat Sourdough (80% Whole Wheat)

A diabetes-friendly sourdough loaf with a softer crumb and easier slicing, adapted from a 100% whole-wheat formula.

Ingredients
Makes one round loaf
  • 400 g whole-wheat flour
  • 100 g bread flour
  • 380–400 g water (start at 380 g)
  • 125 g active sourdough starter (100% hydration)
  • 10 g fine salt
Procedure
Long fermentation with overnight cold proof
  1. Autolyse: Mix all flour with 360 g of the water until no dry flour remains. Cover and rest for 45–60 minutes.
  2. Add starter and salt: Add the sourdough starter and salt. Mix thoroughly. Gradually add the remaining 20–40 g water until the dough is soft and slightly tacky.
  3. Bulk fermentation: Ferment at room temperature for 3½–5 hours. Perform 2–3 gentle stretch-and-folds during the first 90 minutes. End bulk when the dough has risen about 40–50% and shows surface bubbles.
  4. Pre-shape and shape: Turn dough onto a lightly floured surface, pre-shape into a round, rest 15–20 minutes, then shape gently into a tight boule. Place seam-side up in a floured banneton.
  5. Cold proof: Cover and refrigerate for 8–14 hours.
  6. Bake: Preheat a Dutch oven to 475°F. Bake covered for 20 minutes, then uncover, reduce oven to 450°F, and bake another 20–25 minutes until deeply browned. Internal temperature should reach 208–210°F.
  7. Cool: Cool completely on a rack for at least 2 hours before slicing.
Notes: This loaf balances high whole-grain content with improved structure. Long fermentation and cold proofing help moderate glucose response. For best blood-sugar control, enjoy with protein or healthy fat.
