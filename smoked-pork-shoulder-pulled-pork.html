Electric Smoker Baby Back Ribs

Tender, smoky baby backs using a modified 3-2-1 method that works beautifully in an electric smoker.

BBQ Smoker 225°F · 4½–5 hours

Ingredients
For 3 lbs baby back ribs

Ribs

  • 3 lbs baby back pork ribs
  • 1–2 tbsp yellow mustard or olive oil (optional binder)

Dry Rub

  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tsp kosher salt
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp paprika (smoked preferred)
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • ½ tsp chili powder or cayenne (optional)

Wrap Add-ins

  • 2–3 tbsp butter (pats)
  • 1–2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1–2 tbsp apple juice or cider vinegar (optional)
  • 1 tbsp honey (optional)

Optional Finish

  • BBQ sauce for glazing
Equipment
Tools you’ll need
  • Electric smoker
  • Apple or cherry wood chips
  • Heavy-duty aluminum foil
  • Instant-read thermometer
Procedure
Modified 3-2-1 Method
  1. Remove membrane from bone side using a butter knife and paper towel.
  2. Apply optional binder and coat generously with dry rub.
  3. Rest 30 minutes (or refrigerate 2–12 hrs, then return to room temp).
  4. Preheat smoker to 225°F with water pan and wood chips.
  5. Phase 1 — Smoke (2 hrs)
    Smoke bone-side down. Keep lid closed as much as possible.
  6. Phase 2 — Wrap (1½ hrs)
    Wrap tightly in foil with butter, brown sugar, and optional liquid.
  7. Phase 3 — Finish (1–1½ hrs)
    Unwrap and return to smoker to firm bark. Optional sauce during last 20–30 minutes.
  8. Cook until internal temp reaches 195–203°F and ribs pass bend test.
  9. Rest 20–30 minutes before slicing.
Notes
Adjust to preference
  • Shorten wrap to 60–75 minutes for firmer ribs.
  • Extend finish phase for more fall-apart tenderness.
  • Apple/cherry wood gives a milder smoke than hickory.
