Smoked Pork Shoulder (Pulled Pork)

Ingredients
Simple rub, patient smoke.
  • 1 pork shoulder (bone-in or boneless)
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp paprika
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (optional)
  • OR 2 tablespoons of your favorite dry rub mix
Procedure
Plan ahead — this is an overnight-friendly cook.
  1. Combine brown sugar, paprika, black pepper, salt, chili powder, garlic powder, onion powder, and cayenne in a bowl to make the dry rub.
  2. Pat the pork shoulder dry with paper towels.
  3. Apply the dry rub generously over the entire pork shoulder, pressing it into the surface.
  4. Cover and refrigerate overnight, or at least for several hours.
  5. The next day, remove the pork from the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for about 1 hour.
  6. Preheat the smoker to 225°F (about 107°C).
  7. Place the pork shoulder in the smoker, fat side up, allowing the fat to render and baste the meat.
  8. Smoke for approximately 1.5 hours per pound, until internal temperature reaches 195–205°F (90–96°C).
  9. If using a mop sauce, combine the ingredients and mop the pork every hour or so after the first few hours.
  10. Remove the pork from the smoker, wrap tightly in aluminum foil, then a towel, and let rest at least 1 hour.
  11. After resting, pull the pork apart with forks and serve with your favorite BBQ sauce.
Notes: Smoking is more art than science. Each cut is different — low temperature, clean smoke, and time are what work the magic.
