Smoked Baby Back Ribs

Electric smoker · Modified 3-2-1 method

BBQ Smoker 225°F · 4½–5 hours

Ingredients
For ~3 lbs baby back ribs

Ribs

  • 3 lbs baby back pork ribs
  • Binder (optional): 1–2 tbsp yellow mustard or olive oil

Dry Rub

  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tsp kosher salt
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp paprika (smoked preferred)
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • ½ tsp chili powder or cayenne (optional)

Wrap Add-ins

  • 2–3 tbsp butter (pats)
  • 1–2 tbsp brown sugar
  • Optional splash: 1–2 tbsp apple juice or apple cider vinegar
  • Optional: 1 tbsp honey

Optional Finish

  • BBQ sauce for glazing (last 20–30 minutes)
Equipment
What you’ll need
  • Electric smoker
  • Wood chips: apple or cherry (hickory also works—go light)
  • Heavy-duty aluminum foil
  • Paper towels + butter knife (for membrane removal)
  • Instant-read thermometer (recommended)
Preparation
Set yourself up for great bark and even cooking
  1. Remove the membrane (bone side): Slide a butter knife under the silvery membrane, grab with a paper towel, and pull it off.
  2. Apply binder (optional): Lightly coat both sides with mustard or oil.
  3. Season: Mix the rub and apply generously to both sides.
  4. Rest: Minimum 30 minutes at room temp. Best: wrap and refrigerate 2–12 hours (then pull out ~30 minutes before smoking).
Smoker Setup
225°F with a water pan
  1. Preheat smoker to 225°F.
  2. Use a water pan to help keep ribs juicy.
  3. Add wood chips: apple or cherry are ideal for baby backs.
Cooking Instructions
Modified 3-2-1 for baby backs
  1. Phase 1 — Smoke (2 hours @ 225°F):
    • Place ribs bone-side down.
    • Run smoke primarily during the first 60–90 minutes.
    • Avoid frequent peeking (electric smokers recover heat slowly).
  2. Phase 2 — Wrap (1½ hours @ 225°F):
    • Lay ribs on heavy-duty foil.
    • Add butter pats + brown sugar (and optional splash/honey).
    • Wrap tightly and return to smoker for 1½ hours.
  3. Phase 3 — Unwrap & Finish (1 to 1½ hours @ 225°F):
    • Carefully unwrap (hot steam + juices).
    • Return ribs to smoker to firm up the bark.
    • Optional: brush with BBQ sauce during the last 20–30 minutes to set.
    • Optional: bump smoker to 250°F for the last ~20 minutes to tighten the exterior.
Doneness Checks
Use more than one signal
  • Temp: typically 195–203°F near the thickest meat (avoid touching bone)
  • Bend test: lift with tongs — ribs should bend easily and the bark may crack slightly
  • Visual: meat pulls back from bones ~½ inch
Rest & Serve
Finish strong
  1. Tent loosely with foil and rest 20–30 minutes.
  2. Slice between bones.
  3. Serve with sauce on the side (or a light brush if you prefer).
Notes
Tune to preference
  • More bite-through: shorten the wrap phase to ~60–75 minutes.
  • More fall-apart: keep wrap at 1½ hours and extend final phase slightly.
  • Less sweet: reduce brown sugar in the rub to 1 tbsp and omit brown sugar in the wrap.
