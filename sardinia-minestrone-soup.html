Sardinia Minestrone Soup

Adapted from The Blue Zones American Kitchen: 100 Recipes to Live to 100 by Dan Buettner

Soups & Stews Makes: 8 servings Simmer: 1–1½ hrs

Ingredients
Hearty beans + vegetables with simple seasonings.
  • 1/3 lb dried garbanzos (about 3/4 cup), presoaked
  • 1/3 lb dried white beans (about 3/4 cup), presoaked
  • 1/3 cup dried pinto or red beans, presoaked
  • 4–6 stalks celery, chopped (~1/2-inch pieces)
  • 1 1/2 cups potatoes, chopped
  • 4–6 carrots (preferably organic), chopped (~1/2-inch pieces)
  • 1 medium onion (white or yellow), chopped (~1/2-inch pieces)
  • 2 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 4–8 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes or freshly ground black pepper
  • 6–8 cups water or low-sodium vegetable stock (optional)
  • 1 (14 oz) can chopped or stewed tomatoes
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt, to taste
For serving (optional): freshly sliced avocado; freshly grated Parmesan
Procedure
Aromatics first, then a long gentle simmer.
  1. Drain the presoaked beans in a colander. Put them in a bowl with water and microwave for 10 minutes (about the time it takes to chop the celery, carrots, and onion into ~1/2-inch pieces).
  2. Add olive oil to a large pot over low heat. Sauté carrots, celery, onion, garlic, and pepper flakes (or black pepper) until onion is translucent, about 3 minutes.
  3. Rinse and drain the beans again, then add them to the pot along with 6–8 cups water (or vegetable stock). Add tomatoes, potatoes, oregano, and bay leaf.
  4. Slow cook over low heat until beans are tender, about 1 to 1 1/2 hours. Season with salt.
  5. Serve topped with sliced avocado and/or Parmesan, if desired.
Tips:
  • Short on time: Use a pressure cooker for ~25 minutes (lentils cook faster—~5 minutes). Bring to peak pressure, then turn off and let it cool naturally.
  • Even better tomorrow: This minestrone tastes best the next day as flavors meld.
  • Storage: If keeping more than 2 days, freeze it.
