Slow Cooker Split Pea Soup

Comforting slow-cooked split pea soup with ham and vegetables.

Ingredients
  • 1 lb dried split green peas, rinsed
  • ½ onion, chopped
  • 3 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 8 oz cubed ham
  • 1 qt (4 cups) chicken stock
  • 4–6 cups water (as needed to cover)
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2 sprigs parsley, tied together
  • 1 bay leaf (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley, for serving (optional)
Procedure
  1. Place the rinsed split peas in the bottom of a slow cooker.
  2. Add the chopped onion, carrots, celery, garlic, and cubed ham to the slow cooker.
  3. Pour in the chicken stock and enough water to ensure ingredients are covered by about ½ inch.
  4. Tie the thyme, parsley sprigs, and bay leaf together with kitchen twine and add to the cooker.
  5. Cover and cook on high for 4–5 hours or on low for 8–10 hours, until the peas are tender and have split to thicken the soup.
  6. Remove herb bundle; season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with additional chopped parsley if desired.
